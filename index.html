Health lessons from Japan and India, side by side

Two of the world's most food-and-movement-rich cultures approach wellbeing very differently — fermented umami versus layered spice, radio calisthenics versus sun salutations. This page pulls the common threads together before you dive into each topic.

Japan vs India: the everyday basics

A quick side-by-side of how each culture traditionally approaches food and movement.

Comparing wellness fundamentals
AspectJapanIndia
Staple grainShort-grain white or brown riceRice, wheat roti/chapati (varies by region)
Signature fermentMiso, natto, pickles (tsukemono)Curd/yogurt, idli & dosa batter
Flavor philosophyUmami, minimal seasoning, dashi baseLayered spices — turmeric, cumin, chili, coriander
Typical meal shapeTeishoku — small balanced dishes + soupThali — grains, dal, vegetable, curd, pickle
Daily drinkGreen tea / matchaMasala chai / herbal kadha
Traditional movementRajio taiso (radio calisthenics), walking, martial artsYoga, Kalaripayattu, temple-run stair walking
